- vexation :: the state of being frustrated. The GRE tends to bring me to tears in vexation.
- abet :: encourage or assist to do something wrong. I've ended up abetting a lot of people, leading them astray through the means of recommendations.
- acquisitive :: excessively interested in acquiring money. Rich folk are acquisitive and easy to spot, but the extremely rich folk are hard to discern.
- attenuate :: reduce the force, effect or value of. No, I can tell that this painting's value has been attenuated by your vandalism.
- blithe :: showing a casual and cheerful indifference. While carl display a blithe attitude towards the grades, you could tell that he internally was shaking with hostility.
- dawdle :: waste time. I believe that I've ended up dawdling all my time away today.
- dissident :: a person who opposes official policy. Gandhi was a dissident of British India, in that he refused to accept or advocate for the salt law.
- efficacious :: successful in producing a intended result. Losing weight for Bob was an efficacious process, he's lost well over 40 pounds since I've last seen him!
- industrious :: diligent and hard working. That girl is industrious, she never stops working for her dream.
- manacle :: chain. He placed the manacles on her, and from then on, it was a tale too vicious to tell.
- noxious :: harmful, poisonous or very unpleasant. Please check the chemistry lab, I can detect a noxious odor emitting from its confines.
- pernicious :: having a harmful effect. Skydiving is a pernicious affair and yet people do it for the thrill.
- prolix :: containing too many words. While one could say that socializing is good, unnecessarily being prolix with your wording is preposterous.
- veritable :: used for emphasis. The speech given by the black man was veritable about the usage of the N word.
